Fahrenheit

Fahrenheit is a temperature scale proposed by Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit in 1724. Its unit is the degree Fahrenheit. On this scale the melting point of water is 32 ° F and the boiling point is 212 ° F. A difference in 1.8 ° F is equal to a difference of 1 ° C.

Celsius

The Celsius scale, also known as the centigrade scale, is a thermometric scale of the metric system used in most countries in the world. Celsius is a temperature scale named after the Swedish astronomer Anders Celsius.

Kelvin

Kelvin is the base unit of the International System of Units for the thermodynamic temperature quantity. THE kelvin scale was named after Irish physicist and engineer William Thomson (1824–1907), 1st Baron Kelvin, who wrote about the need for an "absolute thermometric scale".
